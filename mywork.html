UTSC Repository

Also known as UTSCRep, UTSC Repository is an online contributive library that allows students to share course material at UTSC. UTSCRep is a fully independent project of mine when it comes to backend web development. From my experience at University of Toronto Scarborough, it is really hard for students, especially first years, to know about a course before taking it, or to get enough resources for the course to perform better. Therefore, I came up with the idea of this website. It took me 6 weeks to implement. This website operates on ASP.NET with MSSQL. To give a slight idea of the effort that was put into this website, it uses hash with salt to secure users' data. Files are stored securely, preventing users from accessing files just with a link. Stored procedures and views are used through the whole website's interaction with the database, allowing maximum security to prevent SQL injections and increasing performance. As small as the website seems to the public, it has an administration panel that gives the Administrator enough power to prevent direct interaction through editing the database. Currently, the website has around 1.2gb of stored data with around 100 users. I consider this website as a complete success. Unfortunately, I do not have enough time to maintain it by adding new features.
